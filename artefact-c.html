Phase 3: Pedagogical Intervention Grade XI Chemistry

Artefact C: Foundational Concepts of Periodicity

Lesson Rationale: Armed with insights from the Critical Incident this intervention fundamentally shifted the power dynamic. Digital tools (Gizmos and Jamboard) were no longer used as the instructor; they were used as evidence boards. Every digital click required a prior verbal justification.

Teacher Implementation

  • Gizmos Simulation: Provided a visual dynamic understanding of atomic radii and electron shielding. The learner was forced to hypothesize outcomes before running the simulation.
  • The Justification Matrix: A digital card sorting game where elements were grouped by properties (electronegativity, mass). Organization was secondary; verbal defense of the placement was primary.

Aggregated Learner Responses

Conceptual Check: Predict Magnesium's behaviour.

Learner: "Magnesium behaves more like Sodium. Because both are metals on the left side their atomic structure means they want to lose outer electrons to become stable."

Jamboard Matrix Insight:

Learner: "I'm grouping Chlorine here because the simulation proved it desperately needs just 1 electron to complete its shell making it highly reactive."

Analysis: The shift is undeniable. The learner is no longer reciting definitions; she is predicting atomic behaviour by explicitly linking macroscopic trends to microscopic atomic numbers.
