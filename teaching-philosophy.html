Teaching Philosophy & Theoretical Base

I perceive education not as the transmission of rigid facts but as the deliberate cultivation of cognitive architecture. My practice is anchored in established educational theory striving to move students beyond algorithmic compliance.

1. Socio Constructivism & Active Processing

Drawing upon Jean Piaget’s (1970) cognitive theories I firmly believe that learners must actively construct knowledge through the processes of assimilation and accommodation. Echoing Jerome Bruner (1966) true mastery is born from discovery. A student simply watching a screen is not learning; they must mentally wrestle with the phenomena to synthesize authentic meaning.

2. Deep vs. Surface Strategies

Guided by Biggs et al. (2022) my classroom is designed to disrupt the comfort of surface learning the dangerous trap of rote replication. My objective is to push learners toward deep cognitive strategies forcing them to interpret data, draw lateral parallels between ideas and verbally justify their chemical reasoning.

3. The TPACK Framework

In an era dominated by EdTech Mishra and Koehler’s (2006) TPACK framework is my guiding metric. Technology must never exist in a vacuum. It requires the seamless synthesis of pedagogical and content knowledge. Digital tools are only as powerful as the explanation based questioning that follows them.
