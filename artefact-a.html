Phase 1: Baseline Context Grade XI Chemistry

Artefact A: Introduction to Dynamic Equilibrium

Lesson Rationale: The objective was to diagnose the learner’s initial understanding of evaporation, condensation and reversibility. Digital multimedia and an interactive Soda Can Activity were deployed not just to entertain but to provoke genuine scientific inquiry before introducing complex mathematical equilibrium.

Teacher Implementation

  • Diagnostic Priming: Zoom screen sharing of a high definition time lapse video detailing phase changes in a closed system to establish baseline vocabulary.
  • Phenomenon Modeling: Live webcam demonstration of pressure manipulation to test intuitive grasp of gas laws.
  • Digital Assessment: Google Forms and Jamboard utilized for instantaneous formative assessment and data gathering.

Target Equation Model

CO₂(g) ⇌ CO₂(aq)

Dynamic Equilibrium Shift

Aggregated Learner Responses

Google Form Q: Explain phase changes in your own words.

Learner: "Evaporation happens when liquid gets heated into gas and condensation happens when gas cools back into liquid form."

Soda Can Verbal Query: Why does fizz rapidly escape?

Learner: "Because the trapped gas inside the can rushes out when the pressure suddenly changes."

Analysis: The learner demonstrated a functional everyday understanding of physical changes successfully linking fizz to pressure indicating an emerging grasp of dynamic shifts.
