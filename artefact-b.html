Artefact B: The Critical Incident Report

Shattering the Illusion of Digital Engagement (The Mole Concept)

The Incident Summary

During an intense online lesson on stoichiometry the learner actively manipulated digital elements and successfully solved a string of numerical practice questions. Initially her high interaction rate suggested mastery.

However the critical incident occurred when the digital scaffolds were removed. I asked a deceptively simple conceptual question: "What physical reality does one mole actually represent?"

The learner became paralyzed. She could execute the algebraic steps flawlessly but was entirely incapable of explaining the scientific reasoning behind the formula. She had memorized an algorithm without comprehending the chemistry.

n = m / M

The learner could perfectly balance the algebra but completely failed to conceptually link the macroscopic reality of Mass (m) to the microscopic absolute of Molar Mass (M).

Reflective Analysis & Theory

The Engagement Illusion

This was a profound wake up call. I realized that correct numerical answers generated via digital mimicry do not equate to actual learning. The engagement was entirely surface level and procedural.

Literature Alignment

This stark realization perfectly echoes Biggs et al. regarding the divide between surface and deep learning. Furthermore as Chi and Wylie (2014) established true cognitive engagement only solidifies when a learner is forced to actively explain their reasoning.
