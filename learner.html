MYP|DP | Chemistry & Physical Sciences

Professional Context

Understanding the learning environment, demographic, and specific challenges of my chosen student.

🧪

Context of Practice

Online One to One Urban Demographics Grade XI (16 years old)

This critical inquiry focuses intensely on a single learner navigating an exclusive online one to one learning environment. My student is a 16 year old in Grade XI from an urban background. She possesses moderate prior knowledge but faces the monumental cognitive leap required to transition from middle school science to pre university Chemistry.

While she presents as a highly active participant, frequently engaging with digital whiteboards, simulations and chat features, I began to identify a profound pedagogical blind spot. Remote learning inherently limits multi sensory feedback. I observed that while she could swiftly input correct objective answers into digital platforms the moment she was prompted to verbally dismantle the why behind her calculations the structural integrity of her understanding collapsed. The screen was acting as a shield for superficial compliance.

In a subject like Chemistry, depending too much on digital tools can be quite tricky. This is because the subject requires students to constantly connect what they can see in real life with the invisible world of atoms and molecules. I realised that when online classes rely heavily on simple drag-and-drop activities or automatic answers, it gives students a shortcut. They skip the actual hard work of scientific thinking. Because of this, I had to change my teaching approach. Instead of just making fun and interactive digital lessons, I started making the tasks a bit more challenging. Now, I make sure to ask the learner to verbally explain the hidden atomic reasons behind the correct answers they get on the screen.
